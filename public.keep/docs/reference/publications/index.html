    Publications#

    GOTM has been extensively used and validated in the scientific literature. Below is a comprehensive list of publications using or describing GOTM, organized chronologically.

    16.1 GOTM Core Development and Theory#

    Foundational Papers:

    1. Burchard & Baumert (1995): “On the performance of a mixed-layer model based on the κ-ε turbulence closure.” J. Geophys. Res. Oceans, 100(C5), 8523-8540. DOI

    2. Burchard et al. (1998a): “Comparing the performance of the Mellor-Yamada and the κ-ε two-equation turbulence models.” J. Geophys. Res. Oceans, 103(C5), 10543-10554. DOI

    3. Burchard & Petersen (1999): “Models of turbulence in the marine environment—a comparative study of two-equation turbulence models.” J. Mar. Syst., 21(1-4), 29-53. DOI

    Key GOTM Theory:

    1. Burchard & Bolding (2001): “Comparative analysis of four second-moment turbulence closure for the oceanic mixed layer.” J. Phys. Oceanogr., 31, 1943-1968. DOI

      • Landmark comparison of turbulence models in GOTM

    2. Burchard (2001): “On the q²l equation by Mellor and Yamada (1982).” J. Phys. Oceanogr., 31(5), 1377-1387. DOI

    3. Umlauf & Burchard (2003): “A generic length-scale equation for geophysical turbulence models.” J. Marine Research, 61, 235-265. DOI

      • Generic Length Scale (GLS) framework unifying k-ε, k-ω, MY models

    4. Umlauf & Burchard (2005): “Second-order turbulence closure models for geophysical boundary layers. A review of recent work.” Cont. Shelf Res., 25, 795-827. DOI

      • Comprehensive review of closure schemes

    5. Burchard et al. (2006): “Description of a flexible and extendable physical-biogeochemical model system for the water column.” J. Marine Systems, 61, 180-211. DOI

      • GOTM architecture and FABM coupling

    Recent Core Advances:

    1. Li et al. (2021): “Integrating CVMix into GOTM (v6.0): A consistent framework for testing, comparing, and applying ocean mixing schemes.” Geosci. Model Devel., 14, 4261-4282. DOI
      • CVMix integration, Langmuir turbulence

    • Biogeochemistry and ecosystems
    • Climate change impacts
    • Numerical methods
    • Arctic and sea ice
    • Sediment dynamics

    The diversity of applications demonstrates GOTM’s versatility and the trust the scientific community places in its turbulence schemes.
