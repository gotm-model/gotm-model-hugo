However, for many applications such simple models are too restrictive. Often different spatial references are involved for the state variables, such as the detritus concentration in the water column, measured in [mmol N m] and the fluff layer concentration at the bed, measured in [mmol N m]. Many biogeochemical processes involve more than two substances such as the photosynthesis where different nutrients (e.g. nitrate and phosphorus) are taken up by phytoplankta and oxygen is produced. The ratios between these substances dissipated or produced are usually fixed, in the example of photosynthesis uptake of 16 mmol m nitrate is connected to an uptake of 1 mmol m phosphorus and a production of 8.125 mmol m oxygen.
